1877
The Original Mid-Annandale FC

The very first Mid-Annandale Football Club is founded in Lockerbie — one of the earliest organised clubs in Dumfries and Galloway. They soon establish themselves as one of the stronger county sides, reaching the final 16 of the Scottish Cup.

1921–23
Back-to-Back League Champions

The reformed Mid-Annandale join the Southern Counties Football League and win the title in their first season, then retain it the following year.

1923
Scottish Football League

An invitation to join the newly formed Third Division of the Scottish Football League is accepted. The expansion folded in 1926, leaving the Mids without a league home.

1923
Record Attendance Set

A crowd of 2,100 packs in for a Scottish Cup tie against Queen of the South on 3 November 1923 — a record that still stands today.

1936
The Original Club Folds

After a sustained period without competitive league football, the original Mid-Annandale FC is dissolved.

1959
Lockerbie Boys Club FC — The Mids Reborn

Football returns to Lockerbie with the formation of Lockerbie Boys Club FC. The side enters the Dumfries Amateur Football League and builds strong community roots over the following decades.

1978
Amateur League Champions

The club's sole Dumfries Amateur Football League title, claimed in the 1977–78 season.

2003
South of Scotland Football League

Mid Annandale step up from amateur football to join the South of Scotland Football League. A new era begins.

2014–15
New King Edward Park Reopens

After sharing with Annan Athletic at Galabank, the redeveloped home ground reopens mid-season, giving the Mids a permanent base in Lockerbie.

2017–18
Best Ever League Finish — Runners-Up

Mid Annandale finish 2nd in the SOSL — their best ever finish — after a 2–0 defeat to Threave Rovers on the final decisive match day. The high point of the modern club's history.

2024
Craig Fraser Appointed Manager

Craig Fraser takes charge in July 2024, leading the team into the 2024–25 and 2025–26 seasons in the South of Scotland Football League.
