1877
The Original Mid-Annandale FC

The original Mid-Annandale Football Club is founded in Lockerbie — one of the earliest organised clubs in the Dumfries and Galloway region. They soon establish themselves as one of the stronger county sides throughout the 1880s and early 1890s, reaching the final 16 of the Scottish Cup.

1894
Original Club Folds

The original Mid-Annandale FC folds in 1894. Over the following years several unsuccessful attempts are made to re-establish a club in Lockerbie, leaving a long gap in organised football in the town.

1921
Return to Football & Southern Counties League

After the end of the First World War a new Mid-Annandale team is reformed. In 1921 the Mids join the Southern Counties Football League and enjoy immediate success, winning the title in their first season and retaining it the following year — back-to-back champions in 1921–22 and 1922–23.

1923
Scottish Football League, Third Division

An invitation to join the new Third Division of the Scottish Football League is accepted. However, this ill-fated expansion of the league folded in 1926, leaving the Mids without a league home. They were eventually accepted into the Scottish Alliance League but the glory days had passed.

1923
Record Attendance: 2,100

A crowd of 2,100 — still the club's record attendance — packs into the ground for a Scottish Cup tie against Queen of the South on 3 November 1923.

1926–27
Scottish Qualifying Cup Winners

Mid-Annandale win the Scottish Qualifying Cup in 1926–27, and were runners-up in 1922–23. They also win the Southern Counties Cup in 1928–29, and enjoy a run of success in the Southern Counties Charity Cup in the early 1930s, winning in 1926–27, 1932–33, 1934–35, and 1935–36.

1936
The Club Folds Again

Despite some cup success in the early 1930s, the club eventually quits Kintail Park and uses various school playing fields. After a final decline they fold for good in 1936, bringing the curtain down on the second chapter of Mid-Annandale's story.

1959
Lockerbie Boys Club FC — The Mids Reborn

Football returns to Lockerbie with the formation of Lockerbie Boys Club FC — the direct ancestor of today's club. The new side enters the Dumfries Amateur Football League and builds a strong community following, eventually winning the league title in 1977–78.

2003
South of Scotland Football League

Mid Annandale step up from amateur football and join the South of Scotland Football League, beginning a new competitive era for the club at a higher regional level.

2014–15
New King Edward Park Reopens

After a period sharing with Annan Athletic at their Galabank ground, the redeveloped New King Edward Park reopens mid-season, giving the Mids a permanent home in Lockerbie once more. Capacity: 1,000.

2017–18
SOSL Runners-Up — Best Ever Finish

Mid Annandale finish runners-up in the South of Scotland Football League — the best finish in the modern club's history. The Mids also win the Southern Counties FA Alba Cup that season, and again in 2019–20, as well as the Wigtownshire & District FA Cree Lodge Cup in 2018–19.

2024
Craig Fraser Appointed Manager

Craig Fraser is appointed first-team manager in July 2024, taking charge ahead of the 2024–25 season with captain Luke Hammond leading the side on the pitch.
